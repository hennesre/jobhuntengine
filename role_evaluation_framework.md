# Role Evaluation Framework

## Evaluation Criteria

### Must-Haves (Automatic No if Missing)
- **Location**: NYC-based or fully remote (no relocation, no hybrid requiring relocation)
- **Product Role**: Must be a Product Manager/Lead/Head/VP position (not Program Manager, not Product Marketing)

### Core Fit Assessment

#### 1. Role Level & Scope (Weight: HIGH)
**Ideal matches:**
- IC: Staff PM, Principal PM, Group PM (senior IC level)
- Leadership: Head of Product, VP Product, Founding PM
- Strong ownership and strategic scope

**Yellow flags:**
- Generic "Product Manager" with unclear seniority
- Heavy emphasis on execution over strategy
- Limited scope or autonomy signals

**Red flags:**
- Junior/Associate PM level
- Strictly executional role
- Product Ops or Program Management disguised as PM

#### 2. AI Focus (Weight: HIGH)
**Strong fit:**
- Core product is AI-powered or AI-native
- Building AI features/products from scratch
- Heavy LLM/ML integration in roadmap

**Moderate fit:**
- Traditional product with AI integration plans
- AI as a feature, not core to product

**Weak fit:**
- AI mentioned as buzzword only
- No clear AI strategy or integration

#### 3. Company Stage (Weight: MEDIUM)
**Preferred:**
- Series A-C startups (with funding runway)
- Big Tech (Spotify, Shopify, Stripe, Google, etc.)
- High-growth scale-ups

**Consider carefully:**
- Pre-seed/Seed (depends on funding and founders)
- Later stage but slow growth
- Post-acquisition/restructuring

#### 4. Compensation (Weight: HIGH)
**Target: $240-300k total comp**
- Base + equity + bonus
- Willing to be flexible for exceptional opportunity
- Must be material upgrade from current $205k/4-day week

**Red flags:**
- Comp range tops out below $200k
- Equity-heavy with unclear value
- "Competitive" with no range

#### 5. Culture Signals (Weight: MEDIUM)
**Green flags:**
- "Ship fast," "move quickly," "bias to action"
- Small team, high autonomy
- Technical founders or product-led company
- Clear product vision

**Red flags:**
- Heavy process emphasis
- "Consensus-driven" (slow decision making)
- Vague about product direction
- Excessive meeting culture

#### 6. Company Health (Weight: MEDIUM)
**Green flags:**
- Recent funding or profitability
- Strong growth metrics mentioned
- Known investors or customers
- Clear market position

**Yellow flags:**
- Quiet on metrics
- Competitive market with weak differentiation
- Recent layoffs or pivots

## Decision Matrix

### STRONG YES - Apply Immediately
- Meets all must-haves
- High-weight criteria (Role Level, AI Focus, Comp) all score strong/moderate fit or better
- Most other criteria positive
- No major red flags

### YES - Worth Pursuing
- Meets all must-haves
- High-weight criteria mostly positive (at least 2 of 3)
- Path to target comp visible
- Minor concerns but overall strong alignment

### MAYBE - Research First
- Meets must-haves
- Mixed signals on high-weight criteria
- Need more information to assess fit
- Suggest: quick company research before deciding

### NO - Skip
- Missing a must-have
- Comp clearly out of range (deal-breaker)
- Multiple red flags on high-weight criteria
- Role/company misalignment with goals

## Output Format

**Role**: [Position Title] at [Company]
**Recommendation**: [STRONG YES / YES / MAYBE / NO]

**Quick Take**: [2-3 sentences covering overall fit, key strengths, and primary concerns if any]

**Next Step**: [Apply now / Research first then apply / Pass]

**Comp Note**: [If available, assessment of comp alignment]

## Example Evaluation

**Role**: Head of Product at Fictive (Series B AI startup)
**Recommendation**: STRONG YES

**Quick Take**: Senior scope with AI-first product and remote flexibility. Strong funding with 2-year runway and clear ownership of product strategy. Comp not listed but likely in range given stage and role level.

**Next Step**: Apply immediately. Research company to personalize outreach.

**Comp Note**: Series B Head of Product typically $250-350k range. Should clarify in first conversation.
